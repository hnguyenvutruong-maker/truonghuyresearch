## Executive Summary
VN-Index rebounded 3.07% in the week ending 13 February, closing at the high of 1,824.09 after opening at 1,769.76. The low at 1,741.37 now stands as the important pre-Tet support level. Liquidity averaged VND 21,860bn/day, enough to confirm the rebound despite the holiday calendar. The tape improved because the market closed at the top of its range, not merely because it bounced.

Foreign-flow data is not available for this week, so no ownership estimate is used. That limits confidence in the rebound. Price action improved, but without foreign-flow confirmation the move should be classified as a technical repair led by domestic demand.

## Vietnam Macro Pulse
DXY was nearly flat at 96.88, USD/VND was stable at 25,910, and WTI fell 2.28% to USD 62.89. Macro was friendlier than the prior week. BTC continued to fade, down 1.80%, while gold slipped 0.57%. The absence of FX or oil stress helped local investors buy the dip.

## VN-Index: Weekly Review
The index reclaimed 1,800 and closed exactly at the weekly high, a constructive signal after the prior week's selloff. Support is 1,741.37; resistance is 1,824.09. A post-Tet break above 1,824 would confirm repair; failure there would leave the market trapped in a lower range.

## Sector Spotlight
Real Estate led at +14.74%, showing that domestic beta came back quickly once the index stabilized. That is useful but also high beta: property-led rebounds can be powerful, yet they need liquidity confirmation to become durable.

## The Week Ahead
The next trading window is distorted by Tet. The clean read is to watch 1,824 after the holiday. A strong reopen above that level would invite a move toward 1,860. A weak reopen below 1,780 would put 1,741 back in play.
