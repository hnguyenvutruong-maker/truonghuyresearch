After opening at 1,841.57, the index pushed to 1,854.62 mid-week, sold off to test 1,817.70, and then ground back to close at 1,844.53 — a textbook inside week that suggests the tape is compressing before a directional break.

## Sector Spotlight: Banking
Banking closed -0.59% w/w — the worst major-sector print outside Utilities and Aviation, and notably soft given the sector's typical positive beta to liquidity. With the SBV's reference framework reportedly unchanged and the SGI Capital cash-scarcity thesis gaining airtime, the bid for rate-sensitive names has thinned.

Key sub-themes:

- **Credit growth picture**: Fresh data unavailable this week. Directional read: bank multiples are compressing on growth uncertainty more than on NIM concerns. We will look to the May credit growth release in the Week Ahead as the binary catalyst.
- **VietinBank Capital's accumulation of Petrosetco (PET)**: A non-trivial strategic move linking state-owned banking capital to the oil services value chain. Read-across: SOE banks (CTG, BID, VCB) are increasingly deploying capital toward industrial/service holdings, not just loan books — a structural shift in how the complex earns incremental ROE and one PE-style investors should track for inbound M&A in non-core subsidiaries.
- **Leverage risk in the broader market**: The margin-call episode on a prominent individual investor is a single-name event, but it reinforces our caution into Q3 on highly levered mid-cap property/consumer names.
- **Valuation context**: Sector P/B estimated around 1.5–1.7x — testing historical support. We see selective entry into the SOE bank complex on weakness, with size discipline given the uncertain credit backdrop.

We will rotate away from banking in next week's spotlight unless the credit growth print materially shifts the thesis.
