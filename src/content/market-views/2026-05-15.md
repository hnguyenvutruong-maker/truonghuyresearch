## Sector Spotlight: Banking

Banking was the week's clear underperformer at -2.34%, dragging index contribution disproportionately given the sector's ~40% VN-Index weight. The sell-off is not earnings-driven — Q1 NIM and credit growth prints across the state-owned cohort (VCB, BID, CTG) had remained constructive. Instead, we attribute the move to three concurrent forces:

1. **Foreign rotation out of SOE banks** — the heaviest foreign selling clustered in VCB, BID, and VPB, consistent with the broader -2,076 bn VND weekly net outflow (*synthetic*). When offshore desks derisk EM, VN banking is the first stop on the exit route.

2. **Margin-call contagion anxiety** — the call margin episode on a high-profile individual account (*"Ông Nguyễn Hùng Cường bị call margin"*) is being read by retail as a leading indicator of forced selling pressure in margin-financed banking longs.

3. **Strategic M&A noise** — VietinBank Capital's emergence as a major shareholder at Petrosetco (per the *"VietinBank Capital trở thành cổ đông lớn tại Petrosetco"* headline) is a positive signal for the bank's investment-banking franchise, but the market is not yet pricing this kind of optionality in.

The counterweight is the EVN deposit story. If 150,000 bn VND rotates from EVN's balance sheet into the commercial banking system, the marginal NIM impact for the top-4 state banks is non-trivial — we estimate +8 to +15 bps of NIM uplift depending on duration mix, which translates to roughly 6–11% EPS upside to 2026 consensus on a static cost-of-funds assumption. We rate Banking as **tactical underweight, strategic overweight** with a 4–6 week horizon.
