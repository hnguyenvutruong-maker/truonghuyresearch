## Executive Summary
VN-Index delivered the first decisive weekly breakout of 2026, closing at 1,867.90, up 4.52% from the 1,787.13 open. The tape was not subtle: the index traded as high as 1,888.33 and never revisited the prior week's low zone. Average daily liquidity rose to an estimated VND 30,264bn, showing that the move had real participation rather than thin holiday follow-through.

Foreign investors were net buyers of VND 548.02bn, with VND 19,733.24bn of buy value against VND 19,185.22bn of sell value. The number is not huge relative to total market turnover, but direction matters. This was the first week in the new archive where price strength and offshore flow pointed in the same direction.

## Vietnam Macro Pulse
DXY rose 0.88% to 99.13, but USD/VND eased 0.25% to 26,230. That divergence helped Vietnam equities: the global dollar was firmer, but the local FX channel did not transmit stress. WTI rose modestly to USD 59.12, gold gained 1.20%, and BTC fell 3.59%, leaving the macro tape mixed but not hostile.

## VN-Index: Weekly Review
The week confirmed the year-opening bid. Support now moves up to 1,775.19, while resistance is 1,888.33. The close at 1,867.90 leaves the market within striking distance of 1,900, so the next move depends on whether investors treat 1,888-1,900 as a take-profit zone or a breakout trigger.

## Sector Spotlight
Banking led at +19.09%, which is exactly the kind of leadership that can pull the index higher. Banks carry index weight, liquidity, and macro signalling value. When banks lead with rising liquidity, investors are usually buying a credit-cycle and domestic-demand story, not just chasing beta.

## The Week Ahead
Bull case: banks keep leading and VN-Index clears 1,900. Base case: the market digests gains between 1,830 and 1,890. Bear case: foreign flow flips negative and the index loses 1,775, which would turn this week into a failed breakout.
