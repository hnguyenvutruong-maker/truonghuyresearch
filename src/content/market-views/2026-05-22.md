## Executive Summary
The VN-Index closed the week at 1,847.00, up 1.15% from 1,826.06, masking a far more fragile underlying tape. Four of five tracked sectors printed red, with Aviation (-2.11%), Banking (-1.54%), and Retail (-1.06%) all in the drawdown, yet a single thematic bloc — VinFamily mobility and adjacent caps — pulled the index off the 1,810.35 weekly low to within 20 points of the 1,867.64 high. Average daily turnover of 19,660 bn VND suggests rotation, not conviction, while reported foreign net buying of 1,911 bn VND requires scrutiny given the divergent sector tape. We initiate Q2-2026 coverage with a neutral-to-cautious bias.

The dong held a tight 25,576 band, marginally stronger by 0.06% WoW against a DXY reading of 104.06 — though we flag explicitly that DXY, USD/VND, gold, WTI, and BTC prints below were not confirmed by real-time feeds this run and should be treated as indicative. For now, the input into equity pricing is benign FX, benign rates, and a Q1 earnings season that is now firmly in the rearview.

## VN-Index: Weekly Review
The week opened at 1,826.06 and tested 1,810.35 within the first two sessions — a 0.86% drawdown that flushed weak hands before the index launched its V-shaped recovery. The 1,810.35 floor now serves as our Q2 opening-week support anchor. From the low, the index rallied 2.02% to close at 1,847.00, just 1.10% shy of the 1,867.64 high.

The candlestick signature — a long lower wick, small body near the high — is technically a hammer continuation, but the internal composition is far less convincing. Breadth was negative in 4 of 5 tracked sectors; the index was carried by VinFamily complex flows. Three headlines converged to drive the bid: (1) VinFast's near-$1B capital raise structure, (2) Pham Nhat Vuong's robotics venture signing with a tier-one global semiconductor group, and (3) the Vingroup-affiliated taxi app reaching #1 in the India App Store transport category within 72 hours. VHM, VIC, and VRE likely did the heavy lifting; we estimate (without constituent data this cycle) that the top 3 names contributed the majority of the 20.94-point weekly gain.

Liquidity of 19,660 bn VND/day is below the Q1 average baseline we observed exiting March. Reported foreign net buying of 1,911 bn VND was marked as a synthetic estimate by the data system — treat with caution. Even so, a single-session headline referencing 900 bn VND of foreign selling on 9/6 (note: this date falls outside our 18–22 May window and reflects a separate reporting cycle) suggests the cross-border flow picture is two-sided and tactical, not a clean rotation back into Vietnam.

## Sector Spotlight: Oil & Gas
Oil & Gas closed the week +2.57%, the only tracked sector in positive territory and a clear relative-strength leader. The driver is mechanically simple: WTI holding the 77-handle, combined with seasonal refining margin tailwinds heading into the summer driving window and the structural underinvestment in midstream capacity that has anchored crude in a 70–85 range for over 18 months.

At the constituent level, we expect PVS, PVD, and BSR to have led the tape, with PVT and VIP participating on the tanker and logistics legs. The 2.57% sector outperformance against a -1.54% banking print is notable — historically these two sectors co-move on growth-sensitive allocation cycles, so the divergence signals that positioning, not macro, is driving the tape.

The regulatory and competitive picture: PVN's upstream capex guidance remains the swing factor for services names, while BSR's gross refining margin sensitivity to the 5–7 USD/bbl Singapore crack spread will determine whether this leadership sustains. Foreign ownership headroom is increasingly binding in PVS and PVD as the room-to-buy ratio compresses below 15% of free float, which could mute incremental bid even on positive prints.

Risks to the call: a sub-75 WTI close on any weekly basis would invalidate the rotation thesis and likely drag Oil & Gas back to flat. Watch the 22-day correlation between DXY and WTI — if it flips positive (dollar and oil rising together), the EM-energy trade unwinds.

## Global Cross-Asset Snapshot
DXY at 104.06 (data not real-time confirmed) implies a stable-to-soft dollar backdrop — supportive for EM, neutral for VND. The synthetic USD/VND print of 25,576, -0.06% WoW, is consistent with that. For Vietnam, this means SBV has policy space to hold rates and the dong carry trade remains attractive to offshore funds.

Gold at 2,384.91/oz — also synthetic — sits below the 2,400 psychological level and roughly 15% off the all-time high zone. A sub-2,400 close suggests real-rate pressure is partially easing, which historically rotates flows out of defensives and back into risk. For Vietnam, this is incrementally positive for gold-exposed names like PNJ, where a board-related transaction headline appeared in this week's feed.

WTI at 77.78 — synthetic — anchors our oil & gas call above. BTC at 66,580.95 (synthetic) keeps crypto in the lower half of its 18-month range, with no incremental flow signal for Vietnam-listed crypto-adjacent plays this week. Two notable global headlines worth flagging: (1) the world's largest startup filing for IPO at a possible $1 trillion valuation — a signal of risk-asset reopening that, if it prices well, sets a constructive template for the VinFast/Pham Nhat Vuong robotics entity; and (2) the largest silver fund buying 40+ tonnes, paired with the largest gold fund selling — a positioning rotation that historically precedes a USD weakening leg, which would compress VND/USD slightly and ease importer margins.

The takeaway: cross-asset is supportive but not euphoric. Vietnam benefits from the tone, not the flow.
