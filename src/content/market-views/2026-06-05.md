## Executive Summary

The VN-Index closed the inaugural week of Q2-2026 essentially unchanged at 1,838.59, down a marginal 0.02% from the 1,838.97 open. The print masks a 37.5-point intraday range (1,862.23 high / 1,824.74 low), producing a textbook indecision candle. Liquidity remained robust at 18,070 bn VND per session, but the tape was two-sided. Technology (+1.72%) and Aviation (+1.64%) carried the index, anchored by VinFast's $1B capital raise and FPT's Thailand deal flow, while Utilities (-0.78%) and F&B (-1.04%) lagged after the arrest of a power-generator's chair and CFO. USD/VND firmed 0.13% to 25,499; foreign flow turned marginally negative at an estimated -35 bn VND on the week (synthetic, real-time data unavailable).

## Vietnam Macro Pulse

The dong traded at 25,499 per USD, +0.13% w/w, a controlled drift consistent with the State Bank of Vietnam's managed-band posture. No daily fix adjustment was telegraphed during the week. The more interesting macro signal sat on the corporate side: state-owned Electricity of Vietnam (EVN) reportedly routed 150,000 bn VND into bank deposits as legacy losses were extinguished — a balance-sheet repair that, if sustained, removes a structural drag on the SOE credit complex. The 50% registration-fee waiver for EVs was also extended through end-2030, a continuation of the consumer-incentive regime that has benefited VinFast's volume ramp. With this being the first weekly note of Q2-2026, we will treat 1,838.59 as our base and 18,070 bn VND as our liquidity benchmark going forward.

## Sector Spotlight: Technology

Technology was the week's clear leader at +1.72%, and the news flow justified the bid. Two anchors drove the move:

**VinFast (VFM):** The EV arm of Vingroup raised nearly USD 1bn in fresh capital during the week, while the group's robotics subsidiary inked a partnership with a tier-1 global semiconductor player. Separately, Pham Nhat Vuong's taxi-hailing app reached #1 in its category on the Indian App Store within three days of launch — a direct read-through to the autonomous-mobility narrative. The combination of capital, semiconductor exposure, and a new geographic market in South Asia makes VFM the highest-beta proxy for the "Vietnam tech re-rating" theme in our coverage.

**FPT (FPT):** Chairman Truong Gia Binh met the Thai PM, after which FPT immediately signed partnerships with two leading Asian counterparties. This continues the company's ASEAN digital-services expansion, where revenue from non-Vietnam markets already exceeds domestic.

The third leg is structural. Press coverage framed AI as the "new face of the stock market" and the catalyst for financial-institution reinvention — a narrative that pulled multiple tech-adjacent names. The technical set-up is clean: Technology has now posted back-to-back weekly gains and is the only sector printing both a higher-high and higher-low on our internal 5-week tracker.
