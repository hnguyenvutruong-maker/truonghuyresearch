## Executive Summary

VN-Index closed the week of June 1–5 at 1,838.90, up **+0.40% WoW** on the back of resilient banking bid and modest foreign re-engagement. Average daily matched value held at 18,940 Bn VND — broadly in line with the 4-week average. Cross-asset tape was mixed: DXY softened (-0.31%), gold eased (-0.42%), while WTI firmed +1.85% on supply discipline. Crypto recovered into the weekend with BTC +1.20%.

- **Banking** (VCB, BID, CTG) contributed the bulk of index points; sector closed +1.6% WoW
- **Foreign flow** turned net positive +1,280 Bn VND — first weekly net buy in 3 weeks
- **USD/VND** anchored at 25,460 — SBV absorbed USD demand without FX reserve drawdown concerns
- **Steel (HPG, HSG)** was the week's laggard at -1.4% on softer export PMI
- **Oil & Gas (GAS, PLX)** led sector gains at +2.3% on Brent retracement

## Vietnam Macro Pulse

The State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) maintained a passive FX stance this week, with USD/VND effectively anchored at the 25,460 reference band. The central bank's OMO was a net drain of ~3,500 Bn VND, telegraphing a mild bias toward tighter interbank conditions as deposit growth tracks credit expansion. Interbank overnight held at 4.30% — 25 bps above the SBV refinancing rate — suggesting banks are positioning for the quarter-end balance sheet.

May CPI is scheduled for release on June 7, with consensus expecting +0.18% MoM / +3.4% YoY (versus April's +3.1%). Core CPI should remain sticky around +2.7% YoY, well within the SBV's 4.5% ceiling. Trade balance for May is likely to print a +$1.8Bn surplus on resilient electronics exports (Samsung, Intel), partially offset by softer garment shipments. Forward, the macro picture is constructive: FDI disbursement YTD is tracking +9% YoY and PMI Manufacturing held at 51.2 (above the expansion line for the seventh consecutive month).

## VN-Index: Weekly Review

VN-Index opened Monday at 1,831.55 and closed Friday at 1,838.90, carving out a 28-point weekly range (high 1,842.10 / low 1,814.20). The session-by-session tape showed a typical "T+0 settlement squeeze" pattern into Thursday's close, with Friday printing a doji after the early rally attempt failed at 1,840.

Volume was the story of the week. The uptick was concentrated in the **banking basket (VCB, BID, CTG, TCB, MBB)**, where foreign flow contributed roughly 35% of the tape. The 20-week MA (not displayed; would require extended chart window) is at 1,795, leaving the index **+2.4% above its medium-term trend**.

Market breadth on HOSE was modestly positive: 240 advancers vs 195 decliners on the week, with the average up/down ratio of 1.23 suggesting a slow rotation rather than a broad-based rally. Mid-caps (VNMidcap +0.8%) outperformed both large-caps (VN30 +0.3%) and small-caps (VNSmallcap -0.2%).

**Foreign flow narrative:** Foreign investors returned as **net buyers of +1,280 Bn VND** for the week, marking the first weekly net buy since the third week of May. Buying was concentrated in **VCB (+320 Bn)**, **FPT (+215 Bn)**, and **HPG (-180 Bn, slight sell)**. The pattern suggests tactical re-engagement following the May correction rather than a structural reallocation — note that YTD foreign flow is still -14,800 Bn VND.

## Sector Spotlight: Banking

Banking was the standout sector this week, closing **+1.6% WoW** and contributing roughly **+0.55 percentage points** of the index's +0.40% weekly change. The driver was a combination of (i) Q2 credit growth tracking above guidance at +5.4% YTD, (ii) sustained NIM expansion as deposit cost lagged lending repricing, and (iii) foreign flow returning to the large-cap state-owned banks.

**Top movers and key drivers:**

- **VCB** (+2.1%): Domestic institutional buying post-AGM reaffirmation of 2026 credit target; Q2 NIM expanded +12 bps QoQ
- **BID** (+1.8%): Q1 NPL ratio held flat at 1.42% despite sector-wide credit acceleration
- **CTG** (+1.5%): Announced $1.2Bn rights issue for capital adequacy (CAR target 13.5% by FY26)
- **TCB** (+2.4%): Continued retail credit growth; consumer loan book +18% YoY
- **MBB** (+0.9%): Lagged on profit-taking after May's outperformance

**Catalyst analysis:** The sector catalyst remains (i) the upcoming SBV circular on credit classification (expected end-Q2), (ii) Q2 earnings season in late-July where consensus expects +13% YoY aggregate net profit growth, and (iii) potential re-rating if FY27 ROE guidance moves above the 17% threshold. The dominant risk is **asset-quality deterioration in real estate-linked loans**, which the SBV has flagged in recent inspections.

**Forward outlook:** Maintain constructive on state-owned banks (VCB, BID, CTG) for their capital adequacy and dividend visibility. Selective on private banks (TCB, MBB, VPB) where valuation premium is justified only by cleanest balance sheets. We continue to avoid the **consumer-credit-heavy tier (OCB, MSB)** into a slowing macro backdrop.

## Global Cross-Asset Snapshot

**DXY** softened to 103.42 (-0.31% WoW) on softer US ISM Manufacturing (49.2, below 50) and a slight pullback in US 10Y yields. Implication for VN: a softer dollar reduces pressure on the SBV to defend the dong band, and we continue to model USD/VND anchored at 25,400-25,600 through end-Q3.

**Crypto** recovered modestly: BTC +1.20% to $68,540 and ETH tracked +0.8%. The catalyst was continued ETF inflows ($1.4Bn weekly net, dominated by IBIT) and the conditional approval of ETH staking ETFs in the US. Implication for VN: limited direct, but positive risk-on read-across for domestic risk assets.

**Commodities:** Gold eased -0.42% to $2,385.10/oz as real yields ticked higher on US data. WTI firmed +1.85% to $78.15/bbl on (i) OPEC+ confirming the production discipline extension into Q3, and (ii) geopolitical risk premium from Middle East tensions. Implication for VN: **higher oil is a marginal headwind for the current account** but a tailwind for **PV Oil (PLX)** and **PV Gas (GAS)** earnings — both of which outperformed this week.

**Tie-back to Vietnam:** The global tape remains supportive — softer dollar + firm oil + constructive crypto risk = benign EM backdrop. The domestic risk premium (VNI ~5.5% earnings yield, ~2.5% real) is attractive vs regional peers.
