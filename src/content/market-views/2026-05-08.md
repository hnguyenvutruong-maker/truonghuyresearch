## Executive Summary
VN-Index resumed the April recovery in the week ending 8 May, closing at 1,915.37, up 2.39% from the 1,870.66 open. The index traded from 1,840.43 to 1,924.95 and closed close to the weekly high, a constructive structure. Liquidity improved to VND 23,780bn/day, confirming stronger participation than the prior holiday week.

Foreign-flow data is not available for this week, so the move is judged through price, liquidity, and breadth. That is an important limitation. The breakout above 1,900 is positive, but ownership quality cannot be confirmed without foreign buy/sell data.

## Vietnam Macro Pulse
DXY fell 0.64% to 97.84 and USD/VND eased 0.36% to 26,260. That was a supportive FX combination. WTI fell 10.34% to USD 95.42, reducing the oil-pressure narrative that had complicated late April. Gold rose 4.45% and BTC was flat-to-positive, leaving a mixed but workable cross-asset backdrop.

## VN-Index: Weekly Review
Support is 1,840.43 and resistance is 1,924.95. The close above 1,900 is the key development: it puts the market back into breakout territory after April's repair. Follow-through now matters more than the initial move.

## Sector Spotlight
Real Estate led at +15.49%, a decisive high-beta signal. This type of leadership can pull retail and margin flows back into the market quickly. The risk is concentration: if property names stall, the index needs banks, securities, or Technology to take over leadership.

## The Week Ahead
Bull case: hold 1,900 and push through 1,925 toward the May high zone. Base case: digest between 1,880 and 1,925. Bear case: fail back below 1,900 with no foreign-flow confirmation, turning the breakout into a bull trap.
