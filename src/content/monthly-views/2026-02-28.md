## Executive Summary
February 2026 was a consolidation month rather than a breakdown. VN-Index closed at 1,880.33, -0.53% MoM from an opening level of 1,890.39. The monthly range ran from 1,741.37 to 1,899.44, while average daily liquidity reached VND 22,575bn across 21 sessions. The price action therefore deserves attention: this was not an empty mark-to-market month.

Foreign investors recorded -6,935.42bn VND net flow, with buy value of VND 22,536.71bn and sell value of VND 29,472.12bn where the local dataset has coverage. Tet timing made the flow sample incomplete, but the covered sessions still show heavy foreign net selling. Banking became the relative shelter while Technology cooled after January leadership. Banking led sector performance at +5.30%, while Technology lagged at -7.80%.

## Macro Regime
USD/VND rose even though DXY was flat, which made FX a local risk variable instead of a global-dollar tailwind. Gold rallied, BTC fell sharply, and WTI moved higher, leaving the index resilient but the cross-asset setup defensive. DXY closed at 97.61 (+0.00% MoM), USD/VND closed at 26,030 (+0.58%), WTI closed at USD 67.02 (+7.85%), gold at USD 5,230.50 (+13.15%), and BTC at USD 65,881.80 (-16.20%).

For Vietnam, the most important macro variables are still USD/VND, oil, and domestic liquidity. A stable currency gives SBV more room to avoid abrupt liquidity tightening. Lower oil helps margins and inflation expectations. But neither variable can fully offset persistent foreign selling if offshore accounts keep reducing exposure into strength.

## VN-Index Monthly Review
The index opened at 1,890.39, printed a monthly high of 1,899.44, fell as low as 1,741.37, and closed at 1,880.33. Support is 1,741.37; resistance is 1,899.44. Those two levels define the next month's tactical map.

The shape of the candle matters as much as the final percent change. A month that closes near the high signals accumulation. A month that tests highs and fades points to distribution or at least hesitation. A month that closes far below the open says investors are reducing gross exposure. In this case, the monthly read should be tied to both sector breadth and foreign-flow quality, not just the index close.

The ownership read is crucial. A rally with foreign buying is easier to trust because price and ownership move together. A rally with foreign selling can continue, but it depends more heavily on domestic liquidity, margin appetite, and policy headlines. A drawdown with foreign selling is the cleanest defensive signal because local and offshore pressure are aligned.

## Sector Rotation Map
Banking leadership was rational because investors wanted liquidity, credit-growth exposure, and earnings visibility. Technology's decline looked like a positioning reset after January rather than a collapse in the longer-term thesis. The leader-laggard spread was 13.10 percentage points, which is wide enough to show that stock and sector selection mattered more than simple index beta.

Sector rotation is the best way to judge whether the index move is healthy. Broad rallies usually include banks, securities, industrials, and consumption. Narrow rallies can still work, but they are more sensitive to crowding. Defensive leadership during a falling month is useful for relative performance, but it does not always mark durable risk appetite.

## Global Cross-Asset Synthesis
The cross-asset message was mixed rather than one-dimensional. DXY and USD/VND define the FX channel, WTI defines the inflation and margin channel, gold defines hedge demand, and BTC gives a read on speculative liquidity. The market needs at least two of those channels to cooperate for a durable upside move.

When FX is stable and oil is falling, Vietnam can absorb more foreign selling. When FX weakens and oil rises, the same foreign selling becomes much more damaging. That is why the monthly setup should be judged as an interaction between macro variables and ownership, not as a standalone VN-Index chart.

## The Month Ahead
March starts with the burden on banks to keep carrying the index or Technology to stabilize. A close above 1,900 would restart the bull case; losing the February low would confirm distribution. The key support is 1,741.37 and the key resistance is 1,899.44. A close through either side of that range should be treated as signal, not noise.

The checklist for the next month is foreign net flow, USD/VND stability, WTI direction, and whether leadership broadens beyond Banking. If foreign selling slows and the index holds support, dips can be bought selectively. If foreign outflow persists and support breaks, the portfolio stance should shift back toward capital preservation.
