## Executive Summary

The VN-Index closed May at 1,863.49, down 0.38% MoM, surrendering a mid-month push that briefly tagged 1,933.11. Price action split cleanly into two halves: a +3.31% / +1.38% rally across the first two weeks, followed by a -2.64% / -1.20% reversal as global risk appetite deteriorated. Average daily turnover held firm at ~VND 26.2tn, signalling that the sell-off was not liquidity-driven. Sector leadership rotated decisively into Oil & Gas (+10.36%) and Utilities (+8.30%), while Retail (-9.17%) and Aviation (-5.31%) lagged on consumer-discretionary de-rating. Cross-asset signals were mixed: WTI collapsed -14.30% on demand worries, the DXY firmed +0.71%, and USD/VND held within 0.1% as the SBV defended stability. The tape prints neutral-to-cautious heading into June, with breadth narrowing despite supportive domestic liquidity.

## Macro Regime

The SBV operated a stable VND regime through May, with the USD/VND cross effectively flat at 26,326 (-0.07%) even as the DXY pushed +0.71% higher. This divergence implies active FX intervention or rate differential absorption by the central bank, consistent with a managed-float posture rather than a free-float revaluation. The VND has held the line despite the dollar's broad strength, suggesting SBV comfort with current levels and adequate reserve coverage.

Credit growth indicators remained constructive on the back of Q1 bank earnings, while headline inflation prints stayed within the State Bank's 4.5% ceiling target, preserving room for an accommodative stance. The VND stability has been a tailwind for FII sentiment, even if foreign flow data for the month remains pending reconciliation. With policy rates steady and FX anchored, the domestic liquidity channel remains the principal macro driver for HOSE beta in the near term.

## VN-Index Monthly Review

May printed a classic "round-trip" pattern. The index opened at 1,870.66 and rallied in the first half, peaking at 1,933.11 mid-month on a +3.31% week (04/05–08/05) that saw breadth expand across Real Estate and Banking, followed by a more measured +1.38% advance (11/05–15/05) that stretched towards the highs. Risk appetite then deteriorated sharply: the 18/05–22/05 week shed -2.64% as global commodity weakness and a softer risk-on tape weighed on cyclicals, and the final week (25/05–29/05) extended losses by a further -1.20%, leaving the index at 1,863.49 — below the May open and just above the 1,840.43 monthly low.

Critically, the drawdown did not impair market plumbing. Average daily liquidity of VND 26.16tn was maintained throughout, indicating that selling was orderly and absorbed without forced deleveraging. The VND stability preserved the VND-denominated P&L calculus for foreign allocators. The monthly range of ~92.7 points (1,840.43–1,933.11) reflects a 4.96% intraday spread — elevated but not extreme — and the close nearer the low than the high underscores the late-month risk-off tilt.

## Sector Rotation Map

Leadership rotated from prior-month consumer and tech favourites into energy and regulated-defensive names. **Oil & Gas (+10.36%)** led the tape, an outcome that appears disconnected from the -14.30% WTI move and likely reflects domestic gas/pipeline operators and refining-margin beneficiaries rather than upstream crude exposure. **Utilities (+8.30%)** extended their multi-month run, consistent with tariff and capacity-approval tailwinds. **Real Estate (+6.85%)** and **Banking (+3.68%)** retained bid, providing the index's defensive ballast.

The laggards told a different story. **Retail (-9.17%)** was the worst performer, signalling consumer-discretionary de-rating that warrants monitoring against May retail sales data. **Aviation (-5.31%)** underperformed, potentially on fare-yield concerns despite lower fuel costs. **Technology (-3.86%)**, **Steel (-3.13%)** and **F&B (-2.79%)** rounded out the bottom half, with the latter two likely catching contagion from the consumer-spend narrative. **Securities (-0.54%)** was a notable mild underperformer given the still-elevated turnover, suggesting the brokerage complex is not yet being rewarded for the volume profile.

The rotation pattern reads as a defensive bid into regulated and energy-adjacent names, funded by de-risking in discretionary and rate-sensitive cyclicals.

## Global Cross-Asset Synthesis

Cross-asset signals were mixed-to-cautious. The headline event was the **WTI -14.30% collapse to 87.36**, a move of scale consistent with either demand-implied weakening, OPEC+ supply normalisation, or both — and one that complicates the EM-energy complex's earnings calculus. **Gold (-1.50%)** gave back a portion of its prior-month premium, but the close at 4,560.50 still sits in historically elevated territory, leaving the metal as a hedge of choice for tail-risk positioning. The **DXY (+0.71% to 98.91)** firmed but failed to break higher, and the muted USD/VND response confirms Asian-EM central banks absorbed the dollar bid without yield-stress spillover. **BTC (-5.88% to 73,579.69)** traded as a high-beta proxy, declining with broader risk appetite rather than asserting safe-haven status.

The net read: global liquidity conditions are stable, but growth-sensitive assets (crude, BTC) are pricing in softening demand. Vietnam's VND stability and intact domestic liquidity provide a partial offset, but the global growth signal is the principal external risk to monitor.

## The Month Ahead

June's setup balances domestic tailwinds against external headwinds. On the positive side, the VND peg remains intact, liquidity at ~VND 26tn/day is structurally elevated versus 2024–25 averages, and the SBV's stable-FX posture preserves the carry argument for foreign allocators. The mid-month MSCI rebalance window and Q2 earnings pre-announcement cycle (especially for Banking and Real Estate) are the principal domestic catalysts.

On the risk side, the -14.30% WTI move warrants close monitoring for contagion into Energy-sector earnings revisions, particularly for upstream-exposed names. The Retail sector's -9.17% drawdown is a leading indicator for May consumer-spend data; a soft print could extend the de-rating. Externally, the DXY's push toward 99 with no VND response leaves the FX channel one-sided — a sudden USD bid could force a defensive SBV response. Watch the 1,840 monthly low as the critical support; a clean break opens the path toward 1,800, while a reclaim of 1,900 would re-establish the May highs as resistance-turned-support. Net positioning for June: neutral with a slight defensive tilt.
