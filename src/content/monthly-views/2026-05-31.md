## Executive Summary
VN-Index closed May at 1,863.49, down -0.38% from the month's open of 1,870.66, after printing a volatile range spanning 92.68 points (1,840.43 low to 1,933.11 high). The month traced a failed breakout: early strength pushed to 1,933 by mid-month before sellers drove a sharp reversal back below 1,870. Dispersion was extreme — Oil & Gas surged 19.04% on WTI's jump to USD 87.36, while Real Estate lagged at -4.82%. DXY softened -1.21% to 98.91, yet USD/VND firmed +0.33% to 26,326, signaling SBV-managed band adjustment rather than USD-driven pressure. Gold rallied +5.16% to USD 4,560, the safe-haven bid intact. The divergence is a clear SBV signal: the central bank is absorbing DXY weakness rather than passing it through to VND, consistent with a managed-band recalibration. The 25,500 reference from Q1 is obsolete; 26,000 is the new operative floor. The next SBV communication on the band will be the dominant policy signal entering Q3.

## VN-Index Monthly Review
May traced a textbook failed-breakout structure. The index opened at 1,870.66, rallied to 1,933.11 by mid-month (a 62-point advance), then reversed sharply to close at 1,863.49 with a monthly low of 1,840.43. The -69-point reversal from high to close is the dominant technical signal: buyers tested 1,930+ and were rejected.

**Week-by-week recap:**
- **Week 1 (04/05 – 08/05):** +3.31%. Strong opening week, index gapped above 1,870 and pushed toward 1,920 on momentum buying and DXY weakness.
- **Week 2 (11/05 – 15/05):** +1.38%. Index tagged 1,933.11 (month high), Oil & Gas surged 19% on WTI spike to USD 105. Breakout attempt in play.
- **Week 3 (18/05 – 22/05):** -2.64%. Failed breakout. Sharp reversal from 1,933 as selling accelerated. Banking (+4.61%) the lone defensive bid on a -2% index tape.
- **Week 4 (25/05 – 29/05):** -1.20%. Continued fade. Index settled at 1,863.49, printing a bearish-reversal candle with a 27-point upper shadow. Oil & Gas extended gains (+6.20% w/w), but breadth ran 2:7 against.

Average daily liquidity ran ~20,603 bn VND across 20 trading sessions, well within the Q2-2026 baseline. Volume confirmed the reversal — the week-3 breakdown printed on a +35% volume spike vs. the prior session.

**Technical levels:**
- Support: 1,840.43 (month low) / 1,800 (psychological) / 1,775 (200-day region)
- Resistance: 1,933.11 (month high) / 1,900 (psychological) / 1,870 (month open, now pivot)

The failed breakout at 1,933 is the defining structure. Until that level is reclaimed, the path of least resistance is lower.

## Global Cross-Asset Synthesis
**DXY at 98.91 (-1.21% m/m):** Dollar weakness should be a tailwind for EM Asia, but USD/VND firmed to 26,326 — SBV is absorbing the tailwind, not passing it through. The decoupling is the signal: domestic liquidity conditions are tighter than DXY implies.

**Gold at USD 4,560.50 (+5.16% m/m):** The dominant safe-haven bid. For EM allocators, gold's role in the hedging toolkit is being repriced higher. Vietnam has no direct gold-equity beta; PNJ (Phu Nhuan Jewelry) is the indirect read on local gold-bullion demand.

**WTI at USD 87.36 (+10.08% m/m):** The operative variable for Vietnam. Positive for upstream earnings (PVS, PVD, BSR), negative for net-importer inflation pass-through. The absolute level, not the monthly delta, drives the trade. Retail fuel pricing on 15-day adjustment cycle is the next catalyst.

**BTC at USD 73,379.69 (-7.06% m/m):** Risk-off in crypto, tracking the broader risk-off tone from mid-month. Correlation with VN-Index remains positive throughout Q2 — the parallel drawdown reinforces the risk-off read.

**USD/VND at 26,326 (+0.33% m/m):** The cleanest actionable signal. VND weakening against a weaker DXY means the move is SBV-managed or domestically driven. The SBV is absorbing the DXY tailwind — that is a tightening signal for local liquidity conditions.
