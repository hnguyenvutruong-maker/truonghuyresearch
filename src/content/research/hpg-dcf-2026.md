## Investment View

Hoa Phat Group is modeled as a cyclical steel leader moving from heavy capex into a higher-utilization phase as Dung Quat 2 ramps. The base-case DCF values HPG at approximately **VND 33,000/share**, implying upside versus the recent market price zone around VND 23,600. The model is built as an academic equity-research exercise and should be read as a transparent valuation framework rather than investment advice.

The thesis rests on three drivers. First, HPG's revenue base expanded materially through 2025 and Q1 2026, with Dung Quat 2 supporting higher steel volume. Second, operating leverage should improve if hot-rolled coil and construction steel spreads normalize while utilization rises. Third, free cash flow should improve after the peak investment period, even though working-capital intensity and steel-price cyclicality remain important risks.

## Key Operating Assumptions

The model uses FY2025 as the starting operating base and projects through FY2030. Revenue is assumed to grow strongly in FY2026 as Dung Quat 2 contributes to volume, then gradually normalize as the ramp matures. Base-case revenue growth steps down from 28% in FY2026 to 4% by FY2030.

EBITDA margin is modeled to improve from the low-teens area toward 15.5% by FY2029-FY2030. This reflects a moderate recovery in steel spreads, better fixed-cost absorption, and operating leverage from higher utilization. The model deliberately avoids a peak-cycle margin assumption because HPG remains exposed to HRC pricing, iron ore, coking coal, import competition, and construction demand.

Capex is kept elevated in FY2026-FY2027 before falling toward maintenance levels. That timing is important: a large portion of the valuation comes not from near-term accounting profit, but from the transition from investment spending to free-cash-flow generation.

## What Matters Most

The most important sensitivity is not the terminal-growth assumption by itself. HPG's valuation is more exposed to the combination of normalized margin and reinvestment intensity. If Dung Quat 2 lifts volume but margins remain compressed, equity value creation is much lower. If margins recover while capex steps down, FCFF expands quickly.

WACC also matters. The base case uses 10.6%, with sensitivity tables from 10.0% to 12.5%. A higher discount rate compresses the target price materially, which is appropriate for a cyclical steel company where earnings visibility is lower than in consumer staples, utilities, or regulated infrastructure.

## Risk Factors

The main downside risks are steel spread compression, weaker construction demand, slower Dung Quat 2 ramp-up, higher raw-material costs, and extended working-capital pressure. HPG also carries execution risk because large integrated steel projects can produce timing mismatches between output, inventory, pricing, and cash conversion.

Policy and trade measures can cut both ways. Anti-dumping protection may support domestic producers, but global steel oversupply and China-linked price pressure remain structural issues. The DCF therefore treats the stock as cyclical value with operating leverage, not as a linear compounder.
